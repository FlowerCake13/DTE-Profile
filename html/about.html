Hi, my name is Lindsay!

From a very young age, I have always had an interest in computers, because they had flashing lights and sparked an endless bound of possibilities. When I was in 4th grade, I was first introduced to a block coding program called Scratch. We were first introduced to the program to create a “Makey Makey” project, where we coded a game that would work with buttons we created made from Play-Doh. At that time, I wanted to create games. I continued my adventure in games and began to play a game called Minecraft.

My interest in Minecraft and coding led me to take a summer school course in 6th grade entitled, My Minecraft: where we learned how to create mods for the game in the language JavaScript. This course was led by the company DevLeague. After this course, I continued with DevLeague to their Junior program for the next three years, until they closed, where I learned Web Development - HTML, CSS, and JS.

Once I got to high school, I wanted to learn more about computers and programming. I eventually built my first PC, which has evolved into the computer that I use today. I continued expanding my knowledge in computer science-related topics, such as programming, cyber security, and artificial intelligence.

I began to expand my connections with others by working on various projects and working with different groups. At Punahou School, I was Tech Support for our yearbook, the co-founder of our computer science and coding club, as well as the founder of the school’s Girls Who Code chapter. Working with these different people has taught me how to collaborate and communicate with different people.

Being able to enjoy games, like Minecraft, through coding, inspired me to join our schools High School eSports Program and work with others to change the way that the club is perceived, including raising money to join the competitive leagues.

After my time in DevLeague, I realized that I enjoyed teaching others Web Development. Through my different clubs, as well as being an AP Computer Science TA, I have been able to enhance and immerse myself in teaching others a topic that I am passionate about.
